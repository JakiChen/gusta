We're fully remote, so that we can alaways be close to you.The online world allows us to work from anywhere, at anytime.

The notion of time and work schedules no longer matters: we work with the cream of the crop regardless of their physical position on earth. Just like Uber doesn’t hire drivers, we don’t hire folks. We work with independent talents.

People who work at Gusta are driven and genuine. We care a lot about each other and about what we do. Together, we work hard and laugh harder. From international collectives to non-existent briefs – the brainstorm never ends.But work is not always about working. All in all, we want people to thrive here at Gusta. We believe you do your best work when you feel your best.

It’s all about being about what you’re about

Bringers of thunder

Changing the game takes talent. And that talent comes from the amazing people we are fortunate enough to call coworkers and friends. We’re building a strong, passionate team of curious people who want to find a purpose in their work and support each other in the process.

Meticulous by birth,
rebels by choice

We often rush headlong into new ventures, even before knowing exactly how. We’d like to believe that we can figure it out and grow along the way. But that doesn’t mean that our work ethics are low. Quite the contrary. And we work very hard to live up to them.

Same same but...

We’re all the same, we’re all different, we’re all equal, we’re all people. We make mistakes and learn from them. And that’s totally fine. We believe in openness, radical honesty and vulnerability. Diversity is our strength. Inclusivity is our culture.

Our Mottos

Avocados of change

Oh snap, we meant to say advocates of change. In the famous words of Charles Darwin: “It is not the strongest of the species that survives, nor the most intelligent. It is the one that is most adaptable to change.”

Take work seriously, but not yourself

We strive to create an environment that fosters a culture of excellence, while remaining fun and ego-free. It’s a million times easier said than done, but it’s worth the effort. Even though we’ll never get it fully right.

Rollercoasters over cruise ships

If there’s one thing we’ve learned looking back, is that a bumpy ride is better than none at all. Even a smooth one pales in comparison. Some of the best work that we’ve done comes from laying the tracks as we go.
